I. PURPOSE & INTENT
The intent of this Policy is to establish a standard process for removal of abandoned
vehicles (bicycles, golf carts, cars, trucks and boats) on the University of South Florida
(USF) Tampa campus.
II. STATEMENT OF POLICY
Abandoned vehicles contribute to an unsightly and unsafe campus environment.
Typically, abandoned vehicles are old, damaged or in disrepair and of little or no value.
III. DEFINITION OF TERMS
A. Abandoned Vehicles: Administrative Services defines abandoned as vehicles that
have been observed without movement for an extended period of time (as determined
by the University Police, Facilities Management and Parking and Transportation
Services) and not cared for (old, rusted, flat tires, deteriorating or in disrepair) or a
vehicle parked on USF property in violation of USF Regulation or Policy.
B. Facilities Management is the department vested with the authority to store, dispose
or transfer the title of the abandoned vehicles.
IV. PROCESS STEPS
In the summer of each academic year, the Grounds Department in Services and the
Maintenance/Enforcement Department in Parking and Transportation Services will
survey university property, except residential areas, and identify abandoned vehicles by
placing a notification tag including date of tag and action to be taken. Housing staff will
tag bicycles in residential areas. Vehicles that have not been moved or fixed thirty (30) days
after being tagged will be removed by Services. Occasionally, vehicles such as bicycles that
do not meet the definition of “abandoned” may have to be removed due to factors such
as obstruction of access, construction or safety or environmental compliance.
A. Bicycles:
1. The serial numbers of all removed bikes will be recorded, and reported to the University
Police Department (“UPD”). Services will keep count of how many bicycles are removed
on an annual basis.
2. All removed bikes will be held in Lot 28, (Facilities Management back area lot) for thirty
(30) days. After thirty (30) days, bikes not claimed will be donated to an official USF bicycle
club or not-for-profit organization, recycled or disposed of at the discretion of Services. In
the event the bicycles are sold, the proceeds shall be placed in an auxiliary account dedicated
to student scholarships or other student support services.
3. The owner of a bicycle who wishes to take his/her bicycle back within the thirty (30) day
period described above should contact the UPD at (813) 974-2628. The UPD will
coordinate access to the bicycle with Services and make arrangements with the owner for
pick up. Verification of ownership may be required.
B. Golf Carts:
1. The serial or registration numbers of all removed university golf carts will be recorded and
used to contact the department/Vice Presidential area responsible for the property. All
removed golf carts will be held in Lot 28 (Facilities Management back area lot). The
responsible Vice Presidential area will have thirty (30) days to claim the property. If,
however, they wish to dispose of it, they must follow the procedure provided at the
following link:
https://www.usf.edu/business-
finance/controller/documents/amreliefrequestapprovals.pdf
2. After thirty (30) days, golf carts not claimed will be disposed, recycled, used for parts or
sold at auction. Any proceeds from the sale of such vehicles will be distributed equally
between Services and the original titled owner of the abandoned vehicle (person or
university college/department).
C. Cars/Trucks:
1. The serial or registration numbers of all removed university cars/trucks will be recorded
and used to contact the department/Vice Presidential area responsible for the property.
The Vice Presidential area will have thirty (30) days to claim the property. If, however, they
wish to dispose of the abandon vehicles, they must follow the procedure provided at the
following link:
https://www.usf.edu/business-
finance/controller/documents/amreliefrequestapprovals.pdf
2. Cars/Trucks not belonging to USF will be identified through Parking and Transportation
Services. Refer to USF4.0010 Parking General Guidelines, Registration, Rates, and
Penalties for details.
3. After thirty (30) days, university cars/trucks not claimed will be disposed, recycled, sold at
auction or used for parts. Any proceeds from the sale of such vehicles will be distributed
equally between Services and the original titled owner of the abandoned vehicle (person or
university college/department).
D. Boats:
1. The serial or registration numbers of all removed university boats will be recorded and used
to contact the department/Vice Presidential area responsible for the property. All removed
boats will be held in Lot 28 (Facilities Management back area lot). The responsible Vice
Presidential area will have thirty (30) days to claim the property. If, however, they wish to
dispose of it, they must follow the procedure provide at the following link:
https://www.usf.edu/business-
finance/controller/documents/amreliefrequestapprovals.pdf
2. After thirty (30) days, university boats not claimed will be disposed, recycled, sold at auction
or used for parts. Any proceeds from the sale of such vehicles will be distributed equally
between Services and the original titled owner of the abandoned vehicle (person or
university college/department). The UPD may use discretion in applying this Policy as
determined by specific facts.
E. Resources:
• To reclaim bicycles contact University Police Department at (813) 974-2628.
• To reclaim cars, trucks or boats contact the Service Center at (813) 974-2750.
